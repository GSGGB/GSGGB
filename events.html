Conference

Date: Saturday March 31, 2018
Time: 8:30 AM - 4 PM
Location: University of Toronto, Medical Sciences Building, Room 3154

Horizons in Cancer Stem Cell Research Conference

Date: Saturday March 31, 2018
Time: 8:30 AM - 4 PM
Location: University of Toronto, Medical Sciences Building, Room 3154
The Global Society for Genetics and Genome Biology (GSGGB) welcomes you to our first annual conference: Horizons in Cancer Stem Cells Research. The aim of our conference this year is to shed light on the pioneering work in cancer research done in Toronto, with an emphasis on cancer stem cells. By bringing together world-renowned experts in various sub-fields of cancer research ranging from cancer genomics, stem cell biology, cancer predisposition syndromes, cell signaling, and metastasis, we hope to illuminate the remarkable complexities of one of the biggest killers of mankind: cancer. The speakers list includes Dr. James Rutka, one of the most published pediatric neurosurgeon-oncologists in the world, and much more. Tickets are free, and in order to confirm your spot in the conference which is limited to 250 individuals, you MUST obtain a ticket on Eventbrite! Breakfast, lunch and beverages are completely free! Please note, that during the 1 hour lunch, there will be a research poster session open to all undergraduate students who have done research involving stem cells, cancer or genetics in general; all participants will have the chance to publish their abstracts to a global undergraduate research journal. If interested in presenting your research at the conference, please contact gsgeneticsgenomebiology@gmail.com with a 250 word max. abstract along with your full name and year of study. All abstracts will be published in JIGG (Journal of Innovations in Genetics and Genomics), a global undergraduate research journal! Poster format and competition details will be provided upon registration to the competition. The research competition is limited to 20 students, so sign up ASAP!

Academic Seminar

Date: Thursday September 22, 2016

Location: University of Toronto, University College, Room 161

Research Seminar with Dr. Christopher Pearson: DNA Repeat Instability Disorders

Date: Thursday September 22, 2016
Time: 4 PM - 6 PM
Location: University of Toronto, University College, Room 161
DNA repeat instability is the cause of over 40 diseases including Huntington's disease, muscular dystrophy & amyotrophic lateral sclerosis. Dr. Pearson is a Senior Scientist at The Hospital for Sick Children, Department of Genetics; an Associate Professor at the University of Toronto, Department of Medical Genetics & Microbiology; and a member of the Canadian Genetic Diseases Network. Dr.Pearson completed a Ph.D. (McGill University, 1994) studying mammalian DNA replication and protein interactions with cruciform-DNAs, proceeded to a post-doc (Texas Medical Center) elucidating the newly identified disease-mutation of trinucleotide repeat instability. During this time, Dr. Pearson discovered the elusive slipped-strand DNAs.
